What is this?
Real-time macro indicators that measure economic health. They're like vital signs — they predict whether markets will trend up or down.
Why it matters
High rates + stretched valuations = headwinds for growth stocks. Low rates + GDP growth = tailwinds. Your portfolio reacts to these forces.
How to use it
Check the health score below. Bullish (70+) = add risk. Bearish (<30) = reduce risk. Use it before major allocation changes.
Examples: Fed raises rates → bonds become attractive, growth stocks struggle. GDP slows → earnings compress, valuations compress. VIX spikes → fear = potential entry point. Gold rises → inflation hedge needed. P/E stretched + rates high = high risk, wait for better setup.

How Outlook helps you decide
The health score (0–100) is a single number that rolls up all 9 macro indicators into one verdict. Instead of juggling fed rates, inflation, valuations, and sentiment separately, you get a clear signal:

Bullish (70+): Tailwinds dominate. Low rates + rising GDP + reasonable valuations. → Increase equity %, add growth stocks.

Constructive (55–69): Mixed signals. Some tailwinds, some headwinds. → Normal allocation, stay balanced.

Neutral (45–54): Evenly balanced. High uncertainty. → Hedge with bonds/gold, reduce leverage.
Cautious (30–44): Headwinds dominate. High rates + stretched P/E + inflation sticky. → Cut risk, raise cash, go defensive.

Bearish (<30): Severe headwinds. Recession risk + valuation crash + high rates. → Aggressive deleveraging, prepare for 20%+ correction.

Tailwind/Headwind breakdown shows which indicators are working for or against you. If 7 tailwinds but 2 headwinds, the score is Bullish. If 4 tailwinds and 5 headwinds, it's Cautious.
